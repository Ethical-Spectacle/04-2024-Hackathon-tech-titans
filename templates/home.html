U.S. Farming: A Tale of Two Scales

Just 6% of U.S. farms are large-scale, generating over 75% of agriculture sales. These 105,384 farms show a market dominated by few. In stark contrast, 74% are small farms with modest sales, facing economic hurdles.(USDA)



The Water Challenge in Traditional Farming

The issue of water wastage in agriculture is profound and well-documented. For instance, it is estimated that around 70% of freshwater withdrawals globally are used for agriculture. However, the efficiency of water use can be low, with about 60% of this water wasted due to leaky irrigation systems, inefficient application methods, and the cultivation of thirsty crops in inappropriate climates(UN Water).​. The agricultural sector, which relies heavily on consistent water supply, is projected to see a rise in irrigation needs by 20% due to climate change by 2080​ (Yale E360).
